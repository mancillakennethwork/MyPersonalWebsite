How Much Does a Website Cost in the Philippines (2026)

If you're planning to get a website built this year, you've probably seen prices all over the place. Here's a no-nonsense breakdown of what websites really cost in the Philippines — and what you actually get for your money.

Introduction

There's no single "correct" price for a website, because every project is different. A simple one-page portfolio isn't the same as a full business site with multiple pages, a booking system, or an online store. That's why website pricing in the Philippines can range anywhere from ₱3,000 to ₱50,000+.

The good news? You don't always need the most expensive option. The right website is the one that fits your goals and budget — and is built well enough to actually help your business grow.

Quick answer: A professional, responsive small-business website typically costs between ₱5,000 and ₱25,000. Simple starter sites can begin around ₱3,000, while complex or e-commerce projects can reach ₱50,000 and beyond.

Cost Breakdown: ₱3,000 – ₱50,000+

Here's a general guide to what different budget ranges usually include. These are ballpark figures — your final quote depends on your specific needs.

TierTypical PriceBest For
Starter ₱3,000 – ₱7,000 Simple 1–3 page site, portfolio, or landing page with basic design.
Standard ₱8,000 – ₱20,000 Multi-page business website, responsive design, contact form, SEO basics.
Advanced ₱21,000 – ₱50,000 Custom features, booking systems, animations, CMS, or larger sites.
Premium / E-commerce ₱50,000+ Online stores, web apps, integrations, and ongoing development.

Note: These ranges reflect freelance pricing. Agencies and large firms may charge more due to overhead.

Factors That Affect Cost

Several things move the price up or down. Understanding these helps you see where your budget is going:

  • Number of pages — More pages mean more design and content work.
  • Design complexity — Custom layouts and animations cost more than templates.
  • Features & functionality — Forms, booking systems, logins, and e-commerce add to the build.
  • Responsiveness — Making sure the site works perfectly on phones and tablets.
  • Content creation — Do you provide text and images, or should the designer help create them?
  • Timeline — Rush projects may carry a premium fee.
  • Ongoing needs — Domain, hosting, maintenance, and updates.

Don't forget the recurring costs

Beyond the build, you'll usually pay yearly for a domain name (around ₱700–₱1,200/year) and hosting (₱2,000–₱6,000+/year). Some designers bundle or arrange these for you.

Tips to Save Money

Want a great site without overspending? Try these:

  • Know your goals upfront — A clear brief prevents scope creep and extra costs.
  • Start small, then grow — Launch the essentials first and add features later.
  • Prepare your content — Supply your own text and images to reduce design time.
  • Use a starter tier — A clean 1–3 page site can be incredibly effective.
  • Bundle services — Combining web design with branding often costs less than doing them separately.
  • Ask for a clear quote — Avoid surprises by getting everything in writing before you start.
My starting price is ₱5,000 for a clean, responsive website — perfect for small businesses and individuals who want to look professional without breaking the bank.

Final Thoughts

A website is an investment, not just an expense. Done well, it works for you 24/7 — building credibility, attracting customers, and growing your business. Focus on value rather than the cheapest option, and partner with someone who understands your goals.
